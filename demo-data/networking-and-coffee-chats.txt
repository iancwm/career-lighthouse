TOPIC: Professional Networking and Coffee Chat Etiquette in Singapore

1. THE "WHY" OF NETWORKING
- The "Hidden Job Market": Many roles in SG finance and consulting are filled via internal referrals before they are ever posted on MyCareersFuture or LinkedIn.
- It is not "bodek-ing" (sucking up); it is professional information gathering.

2. COLD OUTREACH (LINKEDIN/EMAIL)
- Tone: Professional, concise, and respectful of "Seniority" (e.g., Dear Mr./Ms. [Surname] or "Hi [Name]" if the firm has a flat culture).
- The Ask: Request a "15-20 minute chat" rather than "a job." 
- Cultural Barrier: First-gen students often feel they are "disturbing" busy people. Reality: Most professionals expect these requests and see it as part of their "giving back" to the alma mater (NUS, NTU, SMU, etc.).

3. THE COFFEE CHAT LOGISTICS
- Location: Usually around Raffles Place, Tanjong Pagar (MBFC), or City Hall. Common spots include Joe & The Juice, Common Man Stan, or even a quiet Ya Kun if it's more informal.
- Dress Code: "Smart Casual" or "Office Wear." No need for a full suit unless the professional is in Investment Banking/Law, but avoid t-shirts/slippers (the "University look").
- The Bill: The student should always offer to pay as a gesture of respect. However, in 90% of cases, the professional will insist on paying for the student. Do not argue; thank them and move on.

4. THE "REFERRAL" MECHANISM
- A referral is not a "guaranteed job." It is a "guaranteed eyes-on-resume."
- After a successful chat, it is appropriate to ask: "Given my background, would you be comfortable referring me for the [X] role?"

5. FOLLOW-UP
- Always send a "Thank You" note on LinkedIn or Email within 24 hours. Mention a specific thing learned during the chat to show active listening.
