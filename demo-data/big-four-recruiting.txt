TOPIC: Understanding the Big Four and Professional Services in Singapore

1. DEFINITION & DISTINCTION
- The "Big Four" refers to the four largest global professional services networks: PwC, EY, Deloitte, and KPMG. 
- IMPORTANT: In Singapore, students often confuse "Big Four" with the "Local Big Three" banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB). The Big Four are primarily for Audit, Tax, and Advisory, while the local banks are for consumer and corporate banking.
- Target Roles: Audit (checking accounts), Tax (compliance), and Advisory/Consulting (fixing business problems).

2. THE RECRUITMENT TIMELINE (SINGAPORE)
- Peak Season: Applications usually open in August/September for the following year's August intake.
- Off-Cycle Internships: Common in SG during the Jan-May or Aug-Dec semesters. Many students take a "Leave of Absence" (LOA) to complete these, as they are high-weightage for full-time offers.
- "Conversion": Most Big Four firms hire their full-time associates directly from their internship pool.

3. THE SELECTION PROCESS
- Stage 1: Online Application (Focus on GPA and CCA leadership).
- Stage 2: Psychometric Testing (e.g., Pymetrics or logic games). 
- Stage 3: Video Interview (HireVue). Automated questions where the student records themselves.
- Stage 4: Assessment Centre (AC). A group case study where candidates solve a business problem while being observed. 
- Stage 5: Partner Interview. Final "vibe check" and cultural fit assessment.

4. COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS
- "I need an Accounting degree": False. Advisory and Consulting arms actively hire STEM, Humanities, and Law majors.
- "Audit is the only way in": False. While Audit is the largest department, "Advisory" is often more competitive and covers M&A, Cybersecurity, and Management Consulting.
